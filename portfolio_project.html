The brief:

We were required to create a professional web-based portfolio that we could use to show our skills to future employers and other workers within the industry. The website provided us with an opportunity to expand our skillset to areas which are different from our field of study. It meant I was able to learn HTML, CSS and learn how to use frameworks to effectively build a website.

Researching and brainstorming:

Creating my website required plenty of research into the documentation of Bootstrap and HTML to make a good looking and properly functioning website. On top of this I designed a wireframe that is shown in the image in this section. I ended up deviating from the wireframe somewhat as I worked on my website as I realised I wasn't able to do certain things without it not working correctly.

Development process:

The development process was incredibly simple, as it mostly revolved around adding small components and then checking to see if they functioned correctly. Once they worked as intended, I would move onto the next section or add to the original component to achieve a final product that I liked (for example adding the blur and gradient to the hero image).

The final outcome:

As you are able to see, the website functions fully, while also providing a way for me to demonstrate my work in a clean and professional manner, while also allowing me to inject some personality to differentiate it from a generic portfolio website.
